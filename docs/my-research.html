Copyright, Creative Common (CC) Licenses, Fair Use

Copyright protects the original works of creators, giving them the exclusive rights to reproduce, distribute, perform, or display their work. This applies to literature, music, art, software, and more. Copyright is automatic and doesn't require registration.

Attribution (CC BY): Allows others to copy, distribute, remix, and build upon the work, even commercially, as long as the original creator is credited. Attribution-ShareAlike (CC BY-SA): Similar to CC BY, but derivatives must be licensed under identical terms. Attribution-NoDerivs (CC BY-ND): Allows redistribution, commercial and non-commercial, but no modifications or adaptations. Attribution-NonCommercial (CC BY-NC): Allows others to remix, adapt, and build upon the work non-commercially, with proper credit to the creator.

Privacy & Web Privacy Policy

A privacy policy is a legal document that outlines how a website or business collects, uses, stores, and protects user data. It ensures transparency and compliance with data protection laws.

1. Purpose of collection: Collect information for a lawful purpose.
2. Source of information: Collect directly from the individual concerned, unless exceptions apply.
3. Collection methods: Use lawful and fair means of collection.
4. Notice of collection: Inform individuals when collecting their information.
5. Use of information: Only use personal data for the purposes it was collected.
6. Accuracy of information: Ensure data is accurate and up-to-date.
7. Correction rights: Allow individuals to access and correct their data.
8. Storage security: Keep personal data secure from loss or unauthorized access.
9. Retention: Donâ€™t keep information longer than necessary.
10. Use for additional purposes: Only use data for other purposes if permitted by law.
11. Disclosure of information: Disclose data only for the purpose it was collected.
12. International disclosures: Ensure data is protected when disclosed overseas.
13. Unique identifiers: Use unique identifiers carefully and only when necessary.

Web Security

1. Phishing: Attackers trick users into providing sensitive information.
Prevention: Train employees to recognize phishing emails and use multi-factor authentication (MFA).
2. SQL Injection: Attackers exploit vulnerabilities in your database.
Prevention: Use parameterized queries and input validation.
3. Cross-Site Scripting (XSS): Attackers inject malicious scripts into web pages viewed by users.
Prevention: Sanitize user inputs and escape HTML.
4. DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service): Attackers overwhelm your site with traffic to make it unavailable.
Prevention: Use a CDN, load balancer, and web application firewall (WAF).
5. Ransomware: Attackers encrypt your data and demand payment to restore access.
Prevention: Use strong backups, patch vulnerabilities regularly, and employ robust endpoint security.
